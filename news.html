September is national Sickle Cell awareness month. Sickle cell disease is the most common genetic blood disorder and affects approximately 100,000 Americans. More than half of all persons with Sickle Cell Disease will require a blood transfusion in their lifetime and the majority of those require multiple transfusions, using an average of four units each month. Thanks to all you Falcons that donated blood this Month. We do quarterly blood drives here at West, the next one will take place in December. (Friday)
